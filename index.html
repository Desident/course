During an abortive attempt to update the NNTP standard in the early 1990s, a specialized form of NNTP intended specifically for use by clients, NNRP[clarification needed], was proposed[citation needed].

This protocol was never completed or fully implemented, but the name persisted in InterNetNews's (INN) nnrpd program.

In October 2006, the IETF released RFC 3977 which updates NNTP and codifies many of the additions made over the years since RFC 977.

At the same time, the IETF also released RFC 4642 which specifies the use of Transport Layer Security (TLS) via NNTP over STARTTLS.

Usenet was originally designed based on the UUCP network, with most article transfers taking place over direct point-to-point telephone links between news servers, which were powerful time-sharing systems.

Readers and posters logged into these computers reading the articles directly from the local disk.
